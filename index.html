Poiseuille's Law: Bronchiole Radius and Airflow

Explore why small changes in airway radius can cause very large changes in airflow. This is especially useful for understanding narrowed bronchioles in conditions such as chronic bronchitis, asthma, or excess mucus.

The app compares the current radius with a normal reference radius of 2.5 mm.
This visually narrows the airway and reduces the effective radius used in the airflow calculation.

Key relationship

Flow ∝ radius4
Relative airflow = (2.50 / 2.50)4 = 100%
Because radius is raised to the fourth power, halving the radius reduces airflow to about one-sixteenth of normal.
Airflow is normal. The bronchiole is open and resistance is low.
